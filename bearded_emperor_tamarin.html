Bearded Emperor Tamarin

The bearded emperor tamarin is local to the tropical rainforests of Central and South America. They can grow up to ten inches long and weigh a pound and have a long tail of 15 inches. Emperor tamarin fur is grey with yellow spots at the chest. The tail is reddish-brown, and the face, hands and feet are black, and feature a little patch of white fur below their mouth. Emperor tamarins have lengthy white beards that extend to each sides of the shoulders. Emperor tamarins have long nails on all toes besides the large toe. They are omnivorous and can eat fruit, honey, sap, insects, bird eggs, and small reptiles like lizards and tree frogs.
